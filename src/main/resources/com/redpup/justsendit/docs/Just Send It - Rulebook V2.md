# Overall Structure

Just Send It takes place over 3 days (Fri, Sat, Sun). Each day contains some number of rounds, during which players ski/ride, take lifts, and acquire new cards. The player with the most fun over the course of three days wins\!

# Game Setup

* Set up the mountain board using a combination of slope and lift tiles  
* Randomly determine a leader from among the players, then give players starting fun: 10 fun for the leader, 12 for the second player, and so on  
* Each player takes a Friday player card and a starting set of 10 skill cards, then shuffles their deck  
* Reveal 3 random Apres cards and place them on the mountain exit spots  
* Populate the shop with 5 skill cards

## Tile Content

Each mountain tile is either a slope or a lift.

Slopes have the following content:

* Difficulty: Number in range 1-20  
* Grade: Green, Blue, Black, Double Black  
* Snow Condition: Groomed, Powder, Ice  
* Zero to two Hazards: Moguls, Trees, Cliffs  
* Optionally, Slow sign

Lifts have the following content:

* Direction: Top, Bottom  
* Color: Red, Yellow, Cyan, Magenta, Grey  
* Optionally, mountain exit

# Round Structure

Each round consists of players taking turns (see below) and continues until everyone has passed. Play starts with the leader and goes clockwise. At the end of the round, the leader token moves clockwise for the next round.

# Turn Structure

Starting with the leader, each player chooses one of the following:

* Ski/Ride: Choose a down-mountain tile to try to ski/ride  
* Pass: End their round and possibly purchase a new skill card  
* Lift: Ride a lift up the mountain

This continues until everyone has passed. If a player has no cards in hand, they cannot choose to ski/ride (except to a lift tile). Then the round advances and the leader passes clockwise.

## Ski/Ride

The player chooses one of the three down mountain tiles from their current location and moves their player token there. At this point they are trying to ski/ride the tile. The player plays cards from their hand until they either succeed or run out of cards. Played skill cards remain in play until a player passes, at which point they are all discarded. If they run out and have not succeeded, they crash.

The exact order of applying effects of cards is as follows. Most of these steps are skipped most of the time.

1. Terrain and effects that change dice types, values, and counts  
2. Roll dice  
3. All other terrain and effects, including rerolls  
4. Check for and gain wobbles  
5. Check for crash or wipeout  
6. Sum skill and check for success

### Effects that change dice types, values, and counts

Some cards and effects may add, remove, upgrade, or downgrade dice counts. Apply these before rolling any dice. For upgrading and downgrading, the dice order is green → blue → black. This means that green dice cannot be downgraded and black dice cannot be upgraded.

### Terrain

Each snow conditions and hazard has an associated effect on the card played

* Groomed: Nothing  
* Ice: \[After roll\] Gain an additional wobble for each 1 rolled.  
* Powder: \[Before roll\] First card only: Remove your lowest die.  
* Moguls: \[Before roll\] Downgrade your highest die.  
* Trees: \[After roll\] All rolled 5s score 0 skill.  
* Cliffs: \[After roll\] All rolled 2s and 3s score 0 skill.

### Wobbles

For each 1 rolled, a player gains a wobble token. If they then have 3 or more wobbles, they crash\!

### Crash & Wipeout

If a player crashes, they immediately stop skiing/riding. They do not gain any points from the tile they traversed, *even if they would have succeeded*. Additionally, they discard one card from their hand, if they have any remaining. If they have no cards remaining, they wipe out and lose 10 fun. 

### Success

If a player does not crash, they sum the values from all rolled dice, all matching icons (+1 each), and any previously summed values for this tile. If the result meets or beats the tile, they succeed\! They gain fun equal to the tile’s difficulty and their turn ends. Otherwise, their turn continues and they play another card. Alternatively, a player can decide to stop playing cards for this tile, in which case they automatically crash.

### Slow

Players can only enter tiles with slow signs if they have two or fewer skill cards in play.

## Pass

Alternatively, a player may choose to pass. After discarding all cards in play, they reveal all cards remaining in their hand. They may choose to buy up to one card with total study cost equal to or less than their total study value. Each card in a player’s hand is worth 1 study value, \+1 for each symbol on a card that matches the tile they are currently on. (Note that only wild match lifts.)

### The Shop

When a player passes, they buy cards from the Shop. At the start of every round, five cards are shown in the shop; only those five are for purchase during the round. When a player buys a card, do not immediately fill in the resulting spot in the shop. At the end of the round add a “sale” token to every card left in the shop that was not purchased, then remove all cards that have two sale tokens and replenish the shop up to five cards. Sale tokens reduce the cost of a card by 1\.

## Lifts

A player may ski/ride onto a lift tile without playing any cards. They then immediately choose to either ski/ride again (to a further down-mountain tile), pass (and buy up to one card), or ride the lift upwards. To ride the lift upwards, they must discard the listed number of cards. If they do, they may choose to trash up the same number of cards from their discard pile to trash, optionally including the card(s) just discarded.

# End of Day and Apres Cards

When a player ends their turn on a mountain exit tile, they may choose to exit the mountain for the day and claim the associated apres card reward. The first player to arrive receives the full reward, every other player to arrive receives a partial reward. As players leave the mountain on Friday and Saturday, they also claim a Player card for the next day.

Players who are still on the mountain at the end of the day gain no Apres benefits and choose Player cards last. Finally, reveal three new Apres cards.

# Skill Card Content

Every skill card in a player’s deck has the following attributes:

* Name  
* Cost  
  * For cards in the starter deck, 0  
  * Otherwise, some number 5-10  
* Zero to three dice in some combination of green d4s, blue d6s, and black d8s  
* Zero to two bonus icons in any combination of the following, including duplicates:  
  * Grades: Green, Blue, Black, Double Black  
  * Snow Conditions: Groomed, Powder, Ice  
  * Hazards: Moguls, Trees, Cliffs  
  * Wild  
* Text, in exactly one of the following categories  
  * Play  
  * Next  
  * Pass  
  * Lift

## Play

This text takes effect immediately when the card is played during a ski/ride. If it adds, removes, upgrades, downgrades, or sets dice, it happens before dice are rolled. Otherwise, it happens immediately after dice are rolled but before any wobbles or skill is scored. It has no effect during a lift or pass.

## Next

This text takes effect when you play your next card this round during a ski/ride. At that time, it will have the same timing rules as Play above. If has no effect during a lift or pass. At the end of the round, all unresolved Next effects are lost.

## Pass

This text takes effect when you reveal your remaining cards during a pass action. It has no effect during a ski/ride or lift. All pass effects take place when you reveal your remaining cards, in any order.

## Lift

This text takes effect when you discard this card during a lift action. Note that if you do not discard this card when taking a lift action, it does not take effect. It has no effect during a ski/ride or pass.

# Player Card Content

Player cards have the following attributes:

* Name  
* Zero or one bonus icon, from the same list as above  
* Optionally, passive ability text  
* Signature card text, in one of the types of text from the list above
